About This Document

This guide maps the lower division core sequence for the B.A. in Music, coordinating content, pacing, and benchmarks across the three concurrent tracks: Music Theory, Aural Skills, and Keyboard Musicianship. It is intended as a reference for instructors teaching within this sequence, to support cross-track coordination and consistent expectations.

Sequence at a Glance

All three tracks run concurrently within each semester. MUS 108 is a prerequisite for the core sequence and is offered in both Fall and Spring; music majors typically take it in Fall of Year 1. Form and Analysis (MUS 312) and 20th and 21st Century Styles and Techniques (MUS 410) are upper-division courses that draw on the full lower-division sequence.

Course Classifications and Expected Workload

Course classifications follow the CSU Course Classification System. The classification determines both contact hours per unit and maximum class size. Under the CSU credit hour definition, each unit of credit represents one hour of in-class instruction and a minimum of two hours of out-of-class student work per week across a 15-week semester. C10 (music activity, small group) courses carry two contact hours per unit rather than one, reflecting the activity-based nature of the instruction. All hours below are per week during the semester.

End-of-Semester Benchmarks — Spring Year 1

Theory I
  • Spell and identify all triad and seventh chord types in any inversion, with figured bass and roman numerals
  • Work I, I6, V, V6, and V7 through SATB chorale style, figured bass in keyboard style, melodic harmonization, and analysis in real music
  • Apply four-part voice-leading principles: spacing, doubling, voice independence, and avoiding parallel fifths and octaves
  • Identify and write non-chord tones: passing tones, neighbors, suspensions, and anticipations
  • Write simple two-voice counterpoint in first, second, and fourth species
Aural Skills I
  • Sight-sing and take dictation on diatonic melodies in major keys and simple meters
  • Notate pitch and rhythm accurately, including ties and the dotted beat
  • Detect and correct errors in performed and notated examples
  • Read fluently across all fifteen major keys
Keyboard I
  • Play major and minor five-finger patterns and their triads in all common keys
  • Perform all four triad qualities (major, minor, augmented, diminished)
  • Play major scales in tetrachord position with correct fingering
  • Use the damper pedal
  • Sight-read and harmonize at a basic level

MUS 112 / 116 / 119 — Theory II, Aural Skills II, Keyboard II

Fall — Year 2

Second semester of the concurrent core sequence. Theory II moves through diatonic harmony in depth, Aural Skills II consolidates minor mode and introduces harmonic listening, and Keyboard II builds full harmonic vocabulary at the keyboard. MUS 312 — Form and Analysis

Fall — Year 3

Upper-division. Prerequisite: completion of the Theory I–III / Aural Skills I–III sequence. This course applies the harmonic and contrapuntal knowledge of the lower-division sequence to the analysis of complete musical works. The first week consolidates chromatic harmony within an analytical framework before moving into phrase structure, formal types, and sonata form. In place of a midterm, the course uses three group quiz checkpoints distributed across the semester, with a cumulative final exam in finals week (Week 16).

MUS 410 — 20th and 21st Century Styles and Techniques

Spring — Year 3

Upper-division. Prerequisite: MUS 312. This course moves beyond tonal harmony into the analytical and compositional techniques of the 20th and 21st centuries: post-tonal pitch organization, modes, and advanced metric concepts. Karpinski chapters skipped in the lower-division sequence are integrated here alongside the Straus post-tonal content. Assessment is through quiz checkpoints and analytical or compositional projects.
